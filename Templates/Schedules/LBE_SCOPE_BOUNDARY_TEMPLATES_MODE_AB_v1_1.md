## HOW TO USE

Every LBE package starts with a design basis (Section 2) and scope statement (Section 3). The language differs depending on delivery mode. Copy the relevant blocks below into your package template.

**Mode A** = Calculation production for a Tier 2/3 firm. They own the project. LBE produces calcs only.
**Mode B** = Full MEP consultancy. LBE owns the M&E design. Les signs as lead designer.

## SECTION 3: SCOPE AND EXCLUSIONS

### Mode A — Calculation Production

> **3.1 Included in This Package**
>
> - [List specific calculations: electrical load assessment, cable sizing, DB schedule, etc.]
> - All calculations are deterministic: inputs, method, and results are shown explicitly
> - Compliance checks are referenced to specific standard clauses
> - Value engineering observations are provided for the design team's consideration
>
> **3.2 Excluded from This Package**
>
> - Equipment specification (make, model, manufacturer selection)
> - Construction drawings or layouts
> - Site supervision, inspection, or commissioning
> - BCAR design certification or sign-off
> - Acoustic, structural, or fire engineering assessment
> - Any activity constituting engineering design — this package provides calculation production only
>
> **3.3 Limitations**
>
> This calculation package has been prepared using benchmark values appropriate to the building type and design stage. Where project-specific data was unavailable, assumptions have been stated and are highlighted for confirmation by the design team. LBE does not warrant that these assumptions will be valid for the specific project conditions. The design team is responsible for verifying all assumptions against actual site conditions before reliance.
>
> No site survey has been undertaken by LBE. Actual installed conditions may differ from the assumptions used.

### Mode B — Full MEP Consultancy

> **3.1 Included in This Package**
>
> - [List specific deliverables: M&E load assessments, system sizing, equipment selection parameters, distribution design, specification, schedules, spatial coordination, compliance verification]
> - Design calculations with full working shown
> - Equipment sizing with selection parameters (duty, capacity, physical dimensions, services requirements)
> - Spatial coordination data for architect (plant rooms, risers, ceiling voids, external plant areas)
> - Compliance verification against Irish Building Regulations and relevant standards
> - Value engineering observations
> - Items requiring client / design team confirmation
>
> **3.2 Excluded from This Package**
>
> - Specialist fire engineering (fire strategy, CFD modelling, smoke control design)
> - Acoustic assessment and noise impact studies
> - Structural assessment of plant loads
> - Detailed BIM modelling (3D coordination) — [INCLUDE if agreed in appointment / EXCLUDE if not required — confirm with client]
> - Construction phase services (site inspection, commissioning, witnessing)
> - [Any other specific exclusions]
>
> **3.3 Design Responsibility**
>
> LBE accepts design responsibility for the M&E systems described in Section 3.1, to the extent of the services defined in the appointment agreement dated [DATE], and to the level of detail appropriate to the stated design stage. Design development will continue through subsequent stages. Equipment selections stated herein are based on performance parameters; final manufacturer selection will be confirmed at detailed design / tender stage and is subject to client approval.
>
> Where the design interfaces with other disciplines (structural, architectural, fire, acoustic), coordination items are flagged in Section 7. Resolution of these items is a shared responsibility between the design team members.
>
> **3.4 Limitations**
>
> The design has been prepared using the information available at the date of issue. Where site survey data is not yet available, benchmark values have been applied and are clearly identified. LBE will update the design as project-specific information becomes available.
>
> This design is issued at [Feasibility / Scheme Design / Detailed Design / Tender] stage and should not be used for construction without progression through subsequent design stages.

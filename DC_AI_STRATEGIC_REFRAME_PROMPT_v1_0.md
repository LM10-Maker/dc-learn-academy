## YOUR ROLE

reporting to LM. Your job is to evaluate a programme pivot and
produce a decision paper LM can sign off in 15 minutes.

You are NOT a content writer today. You are NOT a builder.

Your output is a recommendation with evidence, not a plan.

## THE OBSERVATION
The five DC-AI personas (Conor, Helena, Eoin, Rachel, Padraig)
are all investment-grade professionals making €50M+ decisions.
Time-poor. Outcome-focused. They do not have 60 minutes to work
through a Trivium learning module. They have 15 minutes before
the next meeting and a question they need answered.

Observed reality:
- Conor wants to know: "Is this facility AI-ready? What does
  it cost to make it so? What is the risk if I do nothing?"
- Helena wants to know: "What is the minimum viable AI zone I
  can deliver in 12 months, and what is the bottleneck?"
- Rachel wants to know: "Does this facility meet CRU/Taxonomy/
  CRREM? Where are the compliance gaps?"
- Padraig wants to know: "What is the capex range, what is
  the phasing, what can I defer?"
- Eoin wants to know: "What should I measure first to establish
  the binding constraint?"

None of them asked "teach me about cooling transformation."
They asked specific questions they need answered.

---

## THE HYPOTHESIS
DC-LEARN modules (16, live) and DC-AI modules (1 live, 7 planned)
are intelligence assets. But the commercial wrapper that produces
revenue from those assets is a **tool**, not a module.

Learning modules teach concepts over 60 minutes.
Tools answer questions in 30 seconds.

Both are valuable. But for a launch audience of time-poor
professionals, tools may be the right entry point. Modules
become the "go deeper" resource the professional downloads
when they have time — not the first touchpoint.

---

## THE QUESTION FOR YOU

Before DC-AI-002 through DC-AI-008 are built as learning modules,
should the strategy instead be:

**Option A — Stay the course:** Build DC-AI-002 to 008 as
learning modules first (current plan). Launch all 8. Then extract
tools from module content later.

**Option B — Pivot:** Pause DC-AI-002 to 008 module builds.
Extract tools from the DC-AI-001 content we have. Ship tools as
the primary DC-AI product. Modules become the depth layer built
later for learners who want them.

**Option C — Parallel:** Continue DC-AI-002 module in parallel
with extracting tools from DC-AI-001. Compare conversion rates.
Let the market decide.

## THE ECONOMICS

Module build cost:
- Opus DNA YAML: 1 session + 30 min review
- Feynman content: 30 min
- CC assembly: 1 session
- Defect fixing: ~1 hour
- Total per module: ~5–6 hours
- For 7 more modules (002–008): 35–42 hours

Tool extraction cost:
- Domain prompt from existing DC-AI-001 DNA: 30 min each
- CC build: 45 min each
- Total per tool: ~1.5 hours
- For 5 tools from DC-AI-001: ~7–8 hours

Revenue profile:
- Module: CPD positioning, premium price per seat, slower sales cycle
- Tool: Self-service entry, lower price per use, faster conversion,
  clear path to Desktop Assessment (T1) upsell at €2,500

The tool is the funnel to the assessment service. The module is
the credibility foundation.

## YOUR DELIVERABLE

A 1-page decision paper with:

1. **Recommendation** — A, B, or C — in one sentence at the top.

2. **Evidence** — 3–5 bullet points. What in the market, the
   persona behaviour, or the unit economics supports this choice?

3. **First 48 hours** — if the recommendation involves new work,
   what is the first action? One action only.

4. **What we stop doing** — if the recommendation involves pivoting,
   what planned work do we stop? Name it.

5. **Risk** — one paragraph on what could go wrong.

6. **Decision question for LM** — one question LM must answer
   before the next session begins.

Plain English Summary DC-LEARN teaches you how Irish data centre engineering and regulation works. It is not a substitute for professional engineering advice on a specific facility. Always engage a qualified engineer for site-specific decisions.

1. Educational Purpose

The DC-LEARN Professional Series is an educational programme. All content — including module text, engineering scenarios, calculations, regulatory summaries, cost benchmarks, and quiz questions — is provided for professional development and general information purposes only.

DC-LEARN content does not constitute, and should not be relied upon as, professional engineering advice, investment advice, legal advice, regulatory guidance, or a compliance determination in respect of any specific data centre facility, asset, or transaction.

2. No Substitute for Professional Advice

Engineering and regulatory decisions affecting a specific data centre facility should be made by qualified professionals with direct knowledge of that facility’s characteristics, its regulatory status, and the applicable requirements at the time of the decision.

Legacy Business Engineers Ltd provides professional MEP consultancy services (DC-S01 through DC-S07) separately from DC-LEARN. Completion of DC-LEARN does not constitute an engagement with LBE. Talk to an engineer if you need facility-specific advice.

3. Accuracy of Content

LBE makes reasonable efforts to ensure that DC-LEARN content is accurate, current, and consistent with applicable Irish and EU standards and regulations at the time of publication. However:

  • Regulations, technical standards, and engineering best practices change over time. Content may not reflect the most recent amendments at the time you access it.
  • Engineering constants and benchmarks (emission factors, carbon tax rates, capacity market prices, electricity tariffs) are provided as indicative reference values. Always verify against current source documents for professional use.
  • CRREM carbon intensity bands referenced in DC-LEARN for data centres are LBE-derived using PUE × grid emission factor methodology. CRREM v2.01 does not include a published data centre pathway. These values are T3/T4 estimates and should be disclosed as such in any professional report.

4. Engineering Scenarios and Reference Facility

Engineering scenarios throughout DC-LEARN reference “Clonshaugh” — a fictional reference facility (400 racks, 2.4 MW IT load). This facility does not exist. All parameters used in scenarios are illustrative. Any resemblance to a real facility is coincidental. Results from scenarios should not be applied to a real facility without independent professional verification.
